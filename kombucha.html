Kombucha

This instructions are based on using 1 gallon glass jar

Starting Kombucha (day 0)

  • Add 2 quarts cold water to glass jar
  • boil water 1 quart of water in pot
  • add 8 black tea bags to pot
    • remove tags from bags
    • tie bags together on string to dip as group
    • dip bags in/out few times as adding
  • Steep Tea for 10 minutes
  • remove bags - do not squeeze bags on way out
  • add 1 cup white sugar to pot and stir
  • pour tea from pot into glass jar
  • let cool to between 68-85 degrees
  • add 1 quart of Kombucha from previous batch
  • add SCOBY
  • store in dark place out of the sun until bottling
  • bottle in 7 - 14 days
    • 7 days - more sweet
    • 14 days - more tangy

check Kombucha (day 7)

Kombucha will get more tangy the longer you leave it

If you like it more tangy, wait longer (up to 14 days) before bottling

Botting

If you like it more tangy, wait longer (up to 14 days)

  • Kombucha will get more tangy the longer you leave it
  • boil water
  • add 3 quarts of boiling water to jar
  • add 8 black tea bags
    • remove tags from bags
    • tie bags together on string to dip as group
    • dip bags in/out few times as adding
    • let steep for 10 minutes
    • remove bags - do not squeeze bags on way out
  • add 1 cup white sugar and stir
  • let cool to between 68-85 degrees
    • on first batch - took over 5 hours to cool down to 80
  • add 1 quart of Kombucha from previous batch
  • add SCOBY
  • store in dark place out of the sun until bottling
  • bottle in 7 - 14 days
    • 7 days - more sweet
    • 14 days - more tangy
  • Bottle when at desired taste
    • it will go from sweet to tangy as it ages
    • taste it at 7-9 days
    • leave 10-14 days if you want more tangy
  • wash and rinse bottles
  • move SCOPY to hotel with 1 quart of kombucha
  • stir Kombucha before bottling because yeast can settle during brewing
  • run kombucha through strainer into measuring cup then pour into bottles
  • add chosen flavour into each bottle
    • Ginger
      • dice up some ginger
      • add 1/2 teaspoon of ginger to each bottle
  • Leave Kombucha in a dark and warm place
  • Chill bottles before opening as it will remove chance of fizzing up

SCOBY needs to stay in hotel for at least 7 days to recover before next batch
