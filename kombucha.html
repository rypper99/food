Kombucha

These instructions are based on using 1 gallon glass jar

Requirements / Preparations

  • 8 bag of black tea
    • remove tags from all tea bags, tie strings together on a longer string to make easier to dip
  • 1 cup sugar
  • SCOBY (that is rested)
  • 1 gallon glass jar
  • 1 pot to boil water
  • platic spoon for stirring
  • bottles
  • clean all equipment that is going to be used

Starting Kombucha (day 0)

  • Add 1 quart cold water to glass jar (this will help it cool the tea quicker when it is added)
  • boil 2 quarts of water in pot
  • Dip tea bags in/out a few times and then leave in the pot
  • Steep Tea for 10 minutes
  • Remove tea bags - do not squeeze bags on way out
  • Add 1 cup white sugar to pot and stir
  • Pour tea from pot into glass jar
  • add 2 cups of Kombucha from previous batch
  • Wait unitl tempature is between 68-85 degrees
  • add SCOBY
  • store in dark place out of the sun

check Kombucha (day 7)

  • Taste Kombucha
    • it will go from sweet to tangy as it ages
    • taste it at 7 days
    • leave it more days if you want more tangy
    • max # days to leave is probably 14 days
  • when at desired taste, proceed to bottling

Bottling

  • Requirements
    • bottles
    • strainer
    • measuring container with pour spot
    • funnel
    • clean bottles and all equipment to be used in bottling
    • jar to hotel SCOBY
    • chosen flavour
  • Move SCOBY to hotel with 2 cups of Kombucha (for next batch)
  • Stir Kombucha before bottling because yeast can settle during brewing
  • Pour Kombucha through strainer into measuring container
  • Add chosen flavour into each bottle
    • Ginger
      • dice up / puree or mash ginger
      • add 1/2 teaspoon of ginger to each bottle
  • Pour from measuring container using funnel into bottle
  • Fill bottle half way up neck
  • Leave Kombucha bottle in a dark and warm place for 2nd ferminatation
  • Chill bottles before opening as it will remove chance of fizzing up

Chill and Serve (7 days after bottling)

Chill bottle in fridge (as it will remove chance of fizzing up) for 24 hours

After 24 hours, taste and check fizziness

If you want more fizziness, leave bottles in a dark warm place to continue fermintation

Once you are happy with the fizziness, move all bottles to fridge to slow down 2nd fermintation

  • boil water
  • add 3 quarts of boiling water to jar
  • add 8 black tea bags
    • remove tags from bags
    • tie bags together on string to dip as group
    • dip bags in/out few times as adding
    • let steep for 10 minutes
    • remove bags - do not squeeze bags on way out
  • add 1 cup white sugar and stir
  • let cool to between 68-85 degrees
    • on first batch - took over 5 hours to cool down to 80
  • add 1 quart of Kombucha from previous batch
  • add SCOBY
  • store in dark place out of the sun until bottling
  • bottle in 7 - 14 days
    • 7 days - more sweet
    • 14 days - more tangy
  • Bottle when at desired taste

SCOBY needs to stay in hotel for at least 7 days to recover before next batch
